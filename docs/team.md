# Team Operating Model

## Founding Structure

The product needs a cross-functional team from day one because trust, identity, and location-aware matching cannot be built safely in a single silo.

## Core Roles

### 1. Product And Company Lead

Owner:

- market direction
- fundraising and partnerships
- vision alignment
- final prioritization

### 2. Product Research Lead

Owner:

- discovery interviews
- persona and JTBD synthesis
- research readouts
- experiment design with product

### 3. Design Lead

Owner:

- onboarding UX
- calm interaction design
- accessibility
- trust and safety UX patterns

### 4. Backend And Platform Lead

Owner:

- APIs
- data models
- auth and permissions
- integration architecture

### 5. Match Intelligence Lead

Owner:

- ranking models
- feature engineering
- feedback loops
- offline and online evaluation

### 6. Trust And Safety Lead

Owner:

- fake-account prevention
- abuse detection
- moderation policy
- safety incident review

### 7. Mobile Or Frontend Lead

Owner:

- client app
- session check-in experience
- candidate discovery flows
- messaging and state transitions

### 8. DevOps And Security Lead

Owner:

- environments
- deployment pipeline
- observability
- secrets and incident response

### 9. QA And Release Owner

Owner:

- test plans
- regression coverage
- release readiness
- launch checklists

## Team Build Order

### Phase 0 To 1

- product lead
- research lead
- design lead
- backend/platform lead

### Phase 2

- match intelligence lead
- frontend or mobile lead
- DevOps/security lead

### Phase 3

- trust and safety lead
- QA and release owner
- part-time legal/privacy advisor

## Ways Of Working

- one product DRI per initiative
- one technical DRI per system change
- weekly research review
- weekly architecture and trust review
- biweekly sprint planning
- lightweight daily async update

## Required Artifacts Per Initiative

- problem statement
- user evidence
- PRD section or update
- trust and abuse review
- technical design note
- test plan
- release checklist

## Decision Rules

- product value decisions: product lead with input from research and design
- security, identity, and abuse decisions: trust lead plus backend or DevOps lead
- ranking decisions: match intelligence lead with product approval
- launch decisions: shared sign-off from product, engineering, trust, and QA

## AI Collaboration Role

Codex can act as:

- documentation lead
- technical planning partner
- implementation support across backend, data, and workflow automation
- release checklist and architecture drafting support

But final accountability for policy, legal, and launch decisions stays with the human founding team.
