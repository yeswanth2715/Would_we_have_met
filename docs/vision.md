# Vision

## Working Vision Statement

Build the safest and most intentional way for socially selective people to discover meaningful connection nearby or online, without forcing them through random events, crowded social scenes, or low-signal interactions.

Help users understand not just who feels right, but why now feels right, including privacy-safe insight into whether they may have crossed paths before or are likely to overlap again soon.

## Problem

Many people are not socially inactive because they lack depth or interest. They are selective. They participate when the context feels right, the energy cost feels worth it, and the other person seems like a genuine fit.

Most current products optimize for:

- volume
- speed
- swiping
- attention loops
- broad exposure

Our product should optimize for:

- timing
- context
- vibe alignment
- trust
- emotional readiness
- safe discovery
- meaningful path and timing insight

## Target User

Primary user:

- introverted or socially selective adults
- low tolerance for noisy, random, or performative social environments
- wants meaningful connection, but with control and emotional safety

Secondary user:

- remote workers or relocated people with thin local networks
- people seeking friendship, dating, or activity companionship in a more thoughtful way
- users who prefer neighborhood-scale discovery over mass-market browsing

## Product Promise

We help users discover people and opportunities that feel right for:

- their current mood
- their available time
- their neighborhood or online context
- their communication style
- their trust and safety preferences
- their comfort level with staying anonymous until mutual trust is earned
- their real-world or routine overlap, explained in a way that feels exciting rather than random

When helpful, the product should be able to say:

"You may have crossed paths on this street before."

"You both pass this building on weekday mornings."

"The match matters now because your openness, timing, and intent align now in a way they did not before."

## Experience Principles

- intent before exposure: ask what the user is open to before showing candidates
- consent before reveal: do not expose sensitive identity or location information too early
- safety before growth: reject fake accounts, suspicious behavior, and low-trust traffic aggressively
- calm over addictive: the product should feel grounding, not chaotic
- useful serendipity: surprising, but not random
- story before data dump: show users a meaningful reason a connection feels real, timely, and plausible
- timing-shift insight: a strong connection may have been nearby in the past, but mutual readiness may only align now
- mediated trust before identity reveal: when users are not ready for direct exposure, the system can help them connect through an AI voice bridge first

## Non-Goals

- becoming a high-volume swipe app
- pushing people into nightlife or event attendance they do not want
- exposing exact live location to strangers
- turning path history into creepy route surveillance
- rewarding fake engagement, vanity metrics, or spammy outreach

## Early Success Metrics

- onboarding completion rate for verified users
- percent of users who report "this felt worth my energy"
- mutual connection acceptance rate
- percent of users who say the connection insight felt exciting, accurate, and non-creepy
- repeat usage from users with low weekly session count but high satisfaction
- fake-account detection precision and recall
- safety incident rate per 1,000 users
