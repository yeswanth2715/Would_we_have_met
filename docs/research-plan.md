# Research Plan

## Objective

Validate whether socially selective users truly want a calmer, trust-first way to discover meaningful local or online connections, and identify the product conditions under which they would actually use it.

## Core Hypotheses

1. Users prefer a small number of high-confidence suggestions over large feeds.
2. Mood and social battery meaningfully change openness to connection.
3. Neighborhood-level relevance matters more than city-wide volume.
4. Users will tolerate stronger verification if it clearly reduces fake accounts and unsafe interactions.
5. Online-first connection can unlock usage when in-person readiness is low.

## Research Questions

- What makes users avoid current social and dating products?
- What level of verification feels acceptable versus invasive?
- How should we phrase mood, energy, and intent so users feel understood?
- What privacy controls are non-negotiable?
- What makes a recommendation feel creepy instead of helpful?

## Methods

- 12 to 15 discovery interviews with target users
- diary study for mood and social-energy patterns over 7 to 10 days
- concept testing with low-fidelity flows
- trust and safety interviews with users who have experienced fake or unsafe profiles
- concierge prototype with manual recommendations before full automation

## Participant Mix

- introverted professionals
- relocated adults building new local networks
- users burned out by mainstream dating apps
- users seeking friendship, not only romance
- mixed gender and mixed age bands within legal adult segments

## Deliverables

- interview synthesis
- pain-point map
- jobs-to-be-done summary
- vocabulary guide for onboarding and mood states
- trust model recommendations
- MVP scope recommendation

## Exit Criteria

- we confirm at least one painful unmet need with repeated evidence
- users accept a trust-heavy onboarding model if the value is clear
- we identify the smallest lovable use case for MVP
- we validate whether friendship, dating, or hybrid discovery should launch first
